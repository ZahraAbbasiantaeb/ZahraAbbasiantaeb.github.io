About Me!

My name is Zahra, and I am currently a second-year Ph.D. student at the Information Retrieval Lab (IRLab) at the University of Amsterdam (UvA), working under the supervision of Dr. Mohammad Aliannejadi and Prof. Dr. Evangelos Kanoulas. I earned my M.Sc. in Artificial Intelligence, specializing in Question Answering systems, and my B.Sc. in Software Engineering, both from Amirkabir University of Technology (AUT).

The topic of my Ph.D. thesis is Conversational Search and Recommendation. I have been co-organized the interactive Knowledge Assistant Track (iKAT) at TREC for two years, aiming to advance personalized conversational search systems. Over the past two years, my work has spanned various aspects of conversational search, including the development of proactive simulators, the application of Large Language Models (LLMs) for evaluation, and improving retrieval performance for complex information needs in conversational search.


Research Interests:
- Conversational Search
- Information Retrieval
- Personalized Search
- Large Language Models
- Evaluation of Conversational Search Systems
- Natural Language Processing
- Question Answering Systems
